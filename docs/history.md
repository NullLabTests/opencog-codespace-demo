# History of OpenCog

## Timeline

### 2001 — Novamente Cognition Engine
Ben Goertzel and colleagues begin developing the Novamente Cognition
Engine (NCE), the precursor to OpenCog. Written in C++, it introduces
the core ideas: a weighted hypergraph knowledge base, probabilistic
reasoning (PLN), and evolutionary program learning (MOSES).

### 2008 — OpenCog Open-Sourced
The Novamente codebase is open-sourced under the name **OpenCog**.
The AtomSpace, CogServer, PLN, and MOSES are released under the
AGPL-3.0 license. The OpenCog project is born.

### 2010 — OpenCog Foundation
The OpenCog Foundation is established as a non-profit to steward the
project. The first OpenCog workshop is held.

### 2013 — Hanson Robotics Integration
OpenCog is integrated into Hanson Robotics' **Sophia** robot as its
cognitive architecture. The AtomSpace serves as Sophia's long-term
memory and reasoning engine.

### 2015 — SingularityNET
Ben Goertzel founds **SingularityNET**, a decentralized AI platform.
OpenCog becomes a core component of the SingularityNET architecture.

### 2018 — AtomSpace v5.0
The AtomSpace reaches version 5.0 with significant performance
improvements: concurrent hash map atom table, refined pattern matcher,
Guile 3.0 support, and RocksDB storage backend.

### 2020 — Hyperon Announced
The **OpenCog Hyperon** architecture is announced as the successor to
OpenCog Classic. The MeTTa language is introduced, combining
functional, logic, and probabilistic programming.

### 2021 — TrueAGI Founded
**TrueAGI Inc.** is founded to develop Hyperon commercially. The
Hyperon codebase moves to `github.com/trueagi-io`.

### 2023 — Hyperon Alpha
Hyperon Alpha is released. The arXiv paper
[*"OpenCog Hyperon: A Framework for AGI at the Human Level and Beyond"*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.18318)
is published with 13 co-authors.

### 2024 — ASI Alliance
The **ASI Alliance** is formed, combining SingularityNET, Fetch.ai,
and CUDOS. Hyperon development accelerates with significant funding.

### 2025 — Hyperon Alpha 1
Hyperon Alpha 1 + ASI:Chain DevNet launched at Web Summit Lisbon
(November 2025). The first practical applications of the new
architecture begin to emerge.

### 2026 — Present
OpenCog Classic (AtomSpace, CogServer) remains actively maintained
by Linas Vepstas. Hyperon / MeTTa development continues at TrueAGI.
Both projects share the same lineage and the same goal: human-level
AGI through a unified cognitive architecture.

## Legacy

The AtomSpace has accumulated **36,000+ commits** from **80+ contributors**
over nearly two decades. It has been deployed in:

- **Robotics:** Hanson Robotics Sophia
- **NLP:** Link Grammar parser, RelEx
- **Knowledge graphs:** ConceptNet integration
- **Blockchain:** SingularityNET infrastructure
- **Research:** Universities and labs worldwide

The project represents one of the longest-running open-source AGI
efforts in existence.
